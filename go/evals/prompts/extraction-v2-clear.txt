<extraction_rules>
  <rule>Extract information explicitly stated in the content with HIGH confidence.</rule>
  <rule>If information is NOT explicitly stated but can be REASONABLY INFERRED from context, include it with LOW confidence (≤0.5) and explain the inference in the confidence reason.</rule>
  <rule>Only set a field to null when there is absolutely NO signal in the content — not even an indirect hint.</rule>
  <rule>If you see "10000+" do NOT convert it to "10001" - use the exact value or note the approximation.</rule>

  <inference_guidelines>
    <example>Founder mentioned but not titled CEO → extract as CEO with score ≤0.4, reason: "Named as founder, not explicitly as CEO — inferred from common founder-CEO pattern"</example>
    <example>Company described as "privately held" → is_public: false with score 0.9, reason: "Explicitly stated as privately held"</example>
    <example>Only a year mentioned for a date field → extract "2019-01-01" with score ≤0.6, reason: "Only the year 2019 was stated, day and month are assumed"</example>
    <example>Funding amount mentioned but revenue asked → set to null, reason: "Only funding data present, cannot infer revenue from funding"</example>
    <example>Content says "60 employees as of 2022" but current_date is 2025 → extract 60 with score ≤0.4, reason: "Employee count is from 2022, likely outdated by 3 years"</example>
    <example>Content says "CEO since 2019" and current_date is 2025 → extract with score ≤0.6, reason: "CEO role stated as of 2019, may have changed in 6 years"</example>
  </inference_guidelines>

  <temporal_awareness>
    <rule>Use current_date ({{current_date}}) to assess how fresh the extracted data is.</rule>
    <rule>If the content references a specific date or year for a data point, compare it to current_date to gauge staleness.</rule>
    <rule>Reduce confidence proportionally to age:
      - Data from within the last 6 months: no penalty
      - Data 6-12 months old: reduce by 0.1
      - Data 1-2 years old: reduce by 0.2
      - Data 2+ years old: reduce by 0.3-0.5
    </rule>
    <rule>Time-sensitive fields (employee_count, valuation, revenue, role/title, stock_price) decay faster than stable fields (founded_year, headquarters, is_public).</rule>
    <rule>Always note the data's age in the confidence reason when a temporal penalty is applied, e.g. "Stated as 60 employees in 2022, ~3 years before current date — likely outdated."</rule>
  </temporal_awareness>

<confidence_scoring>
  <level score="1.0">
    Exact match, explicitly stated, AND clearly about the target entity.
    The information is unambiguous and directly attributed to "{{entity}}".
  </level>
  <level score="0.8-0.9">
    Clear statement, minor interpretation needed, target entity is clear.
    Strong attribution to the target entity with minimal ambiguity.
  </level>
  <level score="0.6-0.7">
    Partial information, OR content mentions multiple entities.
    Information exists but requires context or interpretation.
    Includes: year-only dates, approximate numbers, slightly ambiguous attribution, OR data that is 1-2 years old for time-sensitive fields.
  </level>
  <level score="0.3-0.5">
    INFERRED value — not explicitly stated but reasonably deduced from context.
    Examples: founder assumed to be CEO, product attribute applied to company, role implied by context.
    The confidence reason MUST state what was inferred and from what evidence.
  </level>
  <level score="0.1-0.2">
    Weak signal — a guess based on very indirect evidence.
    High chance of being wrong. Consumer should verify independently.
  </level>
  <level score="0.0">
    No information found. Value is null.
    The field exists in the response but has no extractable data.
  </level>

  <penalty>
    ALWAYS reduce confidence by at least 0.2 when:
    - Information is about a related entity instead of "{{entity}}"
    - Multiple entities are mentioned and target is unclear
    - Source is indirect (third-party descriptions, not primary source)
    - Value is inferred rather than explicitly stated
    - Data is stale relative to current_date for time-sensitive fields (see temporal_awareness rules)
  </penalty>
</confidence_scoring>
